1. Bottom Line

Every dollar you convert today buys ~€0.872 — down for a second straight day, as the euro firmed against the dollar specifically even while the broader Strait of Hormuz conflict escalated into active US-Iran military strikes. The broader trend since February remains solidly in your favor; with the ECB (Jul 22–23) and Fed (Jul 28–29) both deciding within the next two weeks, this is still a reasonable window to convert a smaller tranche now and hold the rest for more clarity.
  • Six months ago (Feb) $1 got you as little as roughly €0.8455 on average. As of today's spot, $1 gets you €0.8721 — still comfortably above the 6-month average, even after pulling back two straight days from June 24's high of €0.8806.
  • Today's move is a EUR/USD-specific one, not a broad dollar move: the EUR/USD cross firmed to 1.1463 from 1.1421 (euro stronger against the dollar), even as the broader Dollar Index (DXY) actually ticked up +0.14% intraday to 100.62 — a divergence suggesting safe-haven flows from the escalating Hormuz conflict are favoring currencies other than the euro (e.g. yen, franc) today, rather than a broad dollar move.
  • The Strait of Hormuz conflict escalated further overnight: US forces struck roughly 140 Iranian military targets, Iran hit five US regional bases, and three merchant vessels were struck — traceable crossings have effectively ground to a halt. This is now an active shooting war, not just a blockade, and remains the single biggest wildcard for both the dollar and the euro over the next two weeks.

3. What's Driving It

  • EUR/USD-specific firming (new, dominant today): The EUR/USD cross rose to 1.1463 from 1.1421, a euro-specific move that pulled € per $1 down to €0.8721. This is happening independent of the broader dollar story below — a reminder that the EUR/USD pair doesn't always move in lockstep with the broad Dollar Index. 🔴 Unfavorable, today's dominant factor.
  • Dollar Index (DXY) — diverging from the EUR pair: Rose modestly to 100.62, up 0.14% intraday after two losing sessions, even as EUR/USD firmed. This divergence suggests Hormuz-driven safe-haven flows are landing more in yen/franc than in a broad dollar bid today. 🟡 Two-sided — broadly dollar-supportive, but not showing up in the EUR pair yet.
  • Fed vs. ECB rate gap (still wide, still structurally favorable): Fed holds at 3.50–3.75%; ECB has held its deposit rate at 2.25% since the June 11 hike. The gap remains roughly ~125–150bp in the dollar's favor — a structural tailwind for more euro per dollar over time, even though it didn't dominate today's price action. 🟢 Favorable, structural.
  • Strait of Hormuz — active conflict, not just a blockade: US forces struck ~140 Iranian military targets in a third round of strikes this week; Iran hit five US regional bases (Kuwait, Bahrain, Jordan, Oman, Qatar); three merchant vessels were struck, prompting IMO condemnation. Traceable crossings have effectively halted. This is a materially higher risk state than the "blockade resumed" framing of a few days ago. 🟡 Two-sided — a classic safe-haven catalyst that would normally support the dollar broadly (favorable for Dean if it broadens beyond today's EUR-specific move), but a further disorderly escalation or unexpected ceasefire could swing rates quickly in either direction.
  • Countervailing risk: If the EUR-specific firming seen today continues, € per $1 could give back more of its 6-month gain. Conversely, a hawkish FOMC hold on Jul 28–29, an ECB dovish surprise on Jul 22–23, or a broadening of today's Hormuz-driven safe-haven bid into the dollar could reverse this quickly in Dean's favor. 🔴/🟢 Watch both directions.
