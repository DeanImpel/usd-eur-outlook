1. Bottom Line

Every dollar you convert today buys ~€0.875 — a modest step back from yesterday, as the euro firmed toward its strongest level since June 19 while the Dollar Index (DXY) stayed roughly flat (~100.92). The broader trend since February remains solidly in your favor (+3.5%); with the ECB (Jul 23) and Fed (Jul 28–29) both deciding within the next two weeks, this is a reasonable window to convert a moderate tranche now and stage the rest around those two events rather than converting the full balance at today's slightly softer rate.
  • Six months ago (Feb) $1 got you as little as roughly €0.8455 on average. As of today's spot, $1 gets you €0.8747 — comfortably above the 6-month average, though down from yesterday's €0.8757 and still short of June 24's high of €0.8806.
  • Today's move reverses yesterday's pattern: EUR/USD firmed to 1.1432 (euro strengthening) even as the Dollar Index held essentially flat at 100.92 — a euro-specific move again, but working against you today rather than for you.
  • European natural gas (TTF) remains elevated above €56/MWh (+29% month-to-date) on intensifying Strait of Hormuz disruption, but that pressure isn't fully offsetting broader euro demand today — a reminder that this transmission channel can swing the rate either direction session to session.
  • The Strait of Hormuz conflict is not just active but escalating — a 10th consecutive night of US strikes on Iran and a fresh tanker attack were reported as of July 20. This remains the single biggest wildcard for both currencies over the next two weeks.

3. What's Driving It

  • Euro strengthening broadly (today's dominant factor): EUR/USD firmed to 1.1432, near its strongest level since June 19, while the DXY held essentially flat at 100.92 — today's € per $1 dip is a euro story, not a dollar story, the same divergence pattern as yesterday but now working against you. 🔴 Unfavorable, today's dominant factor.
  • European gas prices — still elevated, but not driving today's move: TTF natural gas remains above €56/MWh (+15% this week, +29% month-to-date) on Strait of Hormuz-linked LNG supply concerns, yet the euro strengthened anyway today — suggesting broader euro demand (rate-differential positioning ahead of Jul 23) is currently outweighing the energy-cost drag. 🟡 Mixed, a genuine swing factor either direction.
  • Fed vs. ECB rate gap (still wide, still structurally favorable to you longer-term): Fed holds at 3.50–3.75%, next decision Jul 28–29; ECB has held its deposit rate at 2.25% since its June hike, with a hold expected at the Jul 23 decision, though markets now price roughly a 70% chance of a September hike (next scheduled ECB meeting after Jul 23: Sept 9–10) — that repricing is itself euro-supportive and likely explains some of today's broader euro firmness. 🔴 Mildly unfavorable, a headwind building into the Jul 23 decision.
  • Strait of Hormuz — active and escalating, still unresolved: A 10th consecutive night of US airstrikes on Iran and a fresh tanker attack were reported as of July 20, with 56 confirmed incidents and 17 seafarer fatalities cumulative since the Feb 28 blockade began. A classic two-sided risk: further escalation could either strengthen the dollar's safe-haven bid (favorable for € per $1) or keep pressuring European energy costs (which today didn't translate into euro weakness). 🟡 Two-sided, capable of moving the rate sharply in either direction.

5. Two-Week Outlook

Base case: expect roughly €0.860 – €0.885 per dollar over the next two weeks. Today's pullback shows the euro can strengthen even against a Hormuz-driven energy-cost headwind, so the favorable trend is less one-directional than it looked yesterday — the ECB decision on Jul 23 and the FOMC meeting on Jul 28–29 remain the two biggest event risks left in the window, and either could push the rate outside this range.

APEX Take — Conversion Strategy

€ per $1 slipped to €0.8747 today (-0.11%), giving back yesterday's gain as the euro strengthened broadly rather than on the energy-cost-specific softness seen Friday-to-Monday. You're still getting meaningfully more euro per dollar than you were in February (+3.5%), but today's rate isn't the best entry point in the current window, and the Sept-hike repricing at the ECB is a genuine headwind building into Jul 23.

Recommendation: Convert a smaller portion now (e.g., 25–30%) at ~€0.875 per dollar rather than a larger tranche, and hold the remainder to convert after the July 23 ECB decision and July 28–29 FOMC meeting — both fall inside the next two weeks and carry real potential to move the rate meaningfully in either direction, particularly if the ECB confirms or walks back September-hike odds. If your bank or transfer provider offers a forward contract or rate alert/limit order, that remains a better tool than manual timing for the remaining balance.
