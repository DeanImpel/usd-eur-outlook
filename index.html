1. Bottom Line

Every dollar you convert today buys ~€0.876 — a step up from yesterday, as the dollar caught a safe-haven bid from the sharply escalating Strait of Hormuz crisis and the Dollar Index (DXY) rose to ~101.06. The broader trend since February remains solidly in your favor (+3.6%); with the ECB deciding tomorrow (Jul 23) and the Fed next week (Jul 28–29), this is a good window to convert a moderate-to-larger tranche now while the dollar carries the safe-haven premium, staging the remainder around those two events.
  • Six months ago (Feb) $1 got you as little as roughly €0.8455 on average. As of today's spot, $1 gets you €0.8762 — comfortably above the 6-month average and up from yesterday's €0.8747, though still short of June 24's high of €0.8806.
  • Today's move is a dollar-strength story: EUR/USD eased to 1.1413 from 1.1432 as the dollar firmed on safe-haven demand tied to the sharply escalating Strait of Hormuz crisis, with the Dollar Index rising to ~101.06 (+0.14% d/d).
  • European natural gas (TTF) spiked to a fresh four-month high of €59.39/MWh (+1.06% d/d) directly on the Hormuz-driven shipping disruption — a genuine two-sided factor that has, for now, moved in the same direction as the dollar's safe-haven strength rather than offsetting it.
  • The Strait of Hormuz conflict has escalated sharply overnight: traffic has collapsed to ~25 ships/day (from a 49-ship peak Jul 7) and Iran has formally declared the strait "closed until further notice." This is now the single biggest wildcard for both currencies heading into tomorrow's ECB decision.

3. What's Driving It

  • Dollar safe-haven bid (today's dominant factor): The Dollar Index rose to ~101.06 (+0.14% d/d) as the Strait of Hormuz crisis escalated sharply overnight — near-total shutdown of shipping is driving flight-to-safety flows into the dollar. 🟢 Favorable, today's dominant factor.
  • European gas prices spiking to a fresh high: TTF natural gas jumped to €59.39/MWh, a four-month high (+1.06% d/d), directly tied to the Hormuz shipping disruption — adds cost pressure to the eurozone economy, and for now is reinforcing rather than offsetting the dollar's strength. 🟢 Mildly favorable, moving in the same direction as today's dollar bid.
  • Fed vs. ECB rate gap (still wide, still structurally favorable to you longer-term): Fed holds at 3.50–3.75%, next decision Jul 28–29; ECB decides tomorrow, July 23 — a hold at 2.25% is expected, though markets now price roughly a 70% chance of a September hike (next scheduled ECB meeting after that: Sept 9–10). A hawkish tone or explicit September signal tomorrow would be euro-supportive and work against further € per $1 gains. 🔴 Two-sided, key event risk tomorrow.
  • Strait of Hormuz — escalated to near-total shutdown: Traffic has collapsed to ~25 ships/day (from a 49-ship peak Jul 7), Iran has formally declared the strait "closed until further notice," and roughly 6,000 seafarers remain stranded in the Gulf. A classic two-sided risk that is currently favoring the dollar via safe-haven flows — but capable of reversing sharply on any de-escalation or ceasefire headline. 🟢 Net favorable today, watch closely.

APEX Take — Conversion Strategy
