3. What's Driving It

  • Fed vs. ECB rate gap (primary driver, narrowing): Fed holds at 3.50–3.75%, with Chair Kevin Warsh reiterating on July 1 that inflation remains "too high" — a hawkish signal. The ECB has held its deposit rate at 2.25% since the June 11 hike, narrowing the gap to roughly ~125–150bp. Still dollar-favorable overall. 🟢 Favorable, narrowing.
  • Dollar Index (DXY) strength: Sitting near 101.29 today, up from ~100.9 last Friday and 100.97 on July 12 — a firming dollar continues to support your conversion rate. 🟢 Favorable.
  • Strait of Hormuz conflict — ceasefire formally collapsed: On July 12, the U.S. declared the June 17 truce over and revoked the general license authorizing Iranian oil sales; Iran responded by re-declaring the strait "closed until further notice." U.S. forces have now struck roughly 140 Iranian military sites. Maritime risk trackers assess the situation as Critical. This is a bigger escalation than any single-day attack seen so far this cycle, and it's driving safe-haven money into the dollar — more euro per dollar for you. 🟢 Favorable, intensifying.
  • Today's US June CPI release (8:30am ET): Consensus expects headline CPI -0.1% MoM / ~3.9% YoY (on falling gasoline prices) and core CPI +0.2% MoM / ~2.85% YoY. Context: headline CPI has accelerated three straight months (3.3% Mar → 3.8% Apr → 4.2% May) on the Iran energy shock. A hot or in-line print reinforces Fed hawkishness (favorable for you); a cool print revives rate-cut bets (unfavorable). This is today's single biggest swing factor. 🟡 Two-sided, watch closely.
  • Countervailing risk: If the Hormuz conflict de-escalates or a new ceasefire is negotiated, safe-haven dollar demand could unwind quickly, giving back recent gains. A dovish CPI surprise or any ECB signal of a fresh hike at the July 23 meeting would also work against you. 🔴 Watch.

APEX Take — Conversion Strategy

€ per $1 keeps grinding higher (€0.8776, +0.18% since July 12) as the Hormuz ceasefire's formal collapse adds a fresh, genuine risk-off tailwind on top of an already-hawkish Fed. But today is not a low-volatility day to act on: the June CPI print at 8:30am ET is a real two-sided catalyst, and the FOMC meeting (Jul 28–29) has now rolled inside the two-week window alongside the ECB's July 23 decision.

Recommendation: Convert a moderate portion now (e.g., 50%) at ~€0.878 per dollar to bank the current level and the Hormuz-driven gain, and hold the remainder to convert after today's CPI reaction settles and around the July 23 ECB decision and July 28–29 FOMC meeting — both could move the rate meaningfully, and staging avoids converting a lump sum right before an unfavorable surprise. If your bank or transfer provider offers a forward contract or rate alert/limit order, that remains a better tool than manual timing for the remaining tranche.
