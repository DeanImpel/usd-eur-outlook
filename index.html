1. Bottom Line

Every dollar you convert today buys ~€0.876 — about 5.2% more euro than on January 27, this year's worst day for converting dollars to euro. The trend all year has been moving in your favor, and it's still moving that way.
  • Six months ago $1 got you as little as €0.8321. As of July 8, $1 gets you €0.8757 — and the best day so far this year was June 24, when $1 briefly bought €0.8806.
  • The improvement is driven by dollar strength: a hawkish Fed, a wide Fed–ECB interest-rate gap, and safe-haven demand tied to the Strait of Hormuz conflict are all pushing more euro your way per dollar converted.
  • Rate cooled slightly off the June 24 peak but remains well above where the year started — read the chart below as "up = more euro for you."

3. What's Driving It

  • Fed vs. ECB rate gap (primary driver): Fed holds at 3.50–3.75% (June 17, Kevin Warsh's first meeting as Chair) with a hawkish dot-plot flip toward a possible hike — 2026 inflation projections raised to 3.6% headline / 3.3% core. ECB holds at 2.15%. A ~160bp gap keeps the dollar attractive and pushes more euro your way per dollar converted. 🟢 Favorable.
  • Dollar Index (DXY) strength: Broke above 100 (~100.7–101.1), highest since May 2025, on Fed hawkishness plus safe-haven flows. 🟢 Favorable.
  • Strait of Hormuz conflict: Active since Feb 28, 2026. A June 17 US–Iran memorandum briefly calmed markets, but it's unraveling fast — Trump said the ceasefire was "over" July 8, and the US struck Iran again July 9 with Iran retaliating against Bahrain/Kuwait/Qatar shipping. Escalation drives safe-haven money into the dollar, meaning more euro per dollar for you. 🟢 Favorable.
  • US labor data: Strong April jobs prints cut expectations for Fed rate cuts, reinforcing dollar strength — and your euro-per-dollar rate — through Q2. 🟢 Favorable.
  • Countervailing risk: If the Hormuz ceasefire is restored or US inflation cools sharply, the dollar could give back gains quickly and you'd get fewer euro per dollar. This is the main risk to the current favorable trend. 🔴 Watch.

APEX Take — Conversion Strategy

Every driver in play right now — the Fed-ECB rate gap, the Fed's hawkish tilt under new Chair Warsh, and a rapidly escalating Hormuz conflict — is pushing more euro your way per dollar converted. You're already getting meaningfully more euro per dollar than you were in January.

Recommendation: Don't wait for a "perfect" peak — FX timing is a coin flip beyond a few days out, and the risk of a sudden ceasefire or soft CPI print handing back some of that euro-per-dollar gain is real. Convert a meaningful portion now (e.g., 60–70%) at ~€0.876 per dollar to lock in the current favorable rate, and hold the remainder to convert around July 14 (CPI) and July 23 (ECB decision) in case either pushes your rate even higher. If your bank or transfer provider offers a forward contract or rate alert/limit order, that's a better tool than manual timing for the remaining tranche.
