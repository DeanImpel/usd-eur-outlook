1. Bottom Line

Every dollar you convert today buys ~€0.878 — a small step up from yesterday, now that the ECB's rate decision has landed (a hold at 2.25%, no hawkish surprise) and the dollar firmed marginally in its wake. The broader trend since February remains solidly in your favor (+3.9%), and with the ECB catalyst now behind us, the next major event is the FOMC meeting July 28–29 — a reasonable window to convert a moderate tranche now while holding some back to see how the Fed decision lands.
  • Six months ago (Feb) $1 got you as little as roughly €0.8455 on average. As of today's spot, $1 gets you €0.8784 — comfortably above the 6-month average and up slightly from yesterday's €0.8778, though still short of June 24's high of €0.8806.
  • Today's move follows the ECB's decision to hold rates at 2.25% (announced Jul 23) — a low-drama, as-expected outcome that let the dollar firm modestly, with EUR/USD easing to 1.1384 from ~1.1392 the day before.
  • European natural gas (TTF) spiked to €63.92/MWh (+3.16% d/d) — its highest level since January 2023 — as reduced LNG flows from the Persian Gulf intensify competition with Asian buyers. This is a structural cost pressure on the eurozone economy that argues against the euro over time.
  • The Strait of Hormuz conflict remains critical and unchanged since yesterday: non-Iranian ship transits remain near a 90% YoY traffic decline. This stays the single biggest wildcard for both currencies, with the FOMC meeting (Jul 28–29) now the next scheduled catalyst.

3. What's Driving It

  • ECB decision — resolved, muted impact: The ECB held rates at 2.25% as expected (decision Jul 23), with no explicit September-hike signal strong enough to move markets sharply. That removes the week's biggest live event risk. Next scheduled ECB meeting: Sept 9–10. 🟡 Neutral now that it's behind us.
  • Dollar pulling back modestly: The Dollar Index eased to ~100.9 from the ~101.1 cycle-high area, trading in a choppy 100.6–101.1 range with no clear breakout, even as Hormuz-driven safe-haven demand persists structurally. 🟢 Mildly favorable for you.
  • European gas prices — fresh spike today: TTF natural gas jumped to €63.92/MWh (+3.16% d/d), its highest level since January 2023, up more than 45% since the start of July on reduced Persian Gulf LNG flows. 🔴 A growing cost drag on the eurozone economy — unfavorable for the euro over time, though not today's direct price driver.
  • Fed vs. ECB rate gap — Fed now the dominant near-term catalyst: Fed holds at 3.50–3.75%, next decision Jul 28–29; ECB has already acted (hold at 2.25%). A hawkish Fed hold/statement would be favorable for you; any dovish pivot on rate-cut timing would work against further gains. 🟡 Two-sided, the key event risk over the next week.
  • Strait of Hormuz — unchanged, still near-total shutdown: Non-Iranian ship transits remain down ~90% YoY as of the last confirmed read (Jul 21), with no new escalation or de-escalation reported today. Further escalation typically favors the dollar via safe-haven flows; any de-escalation could reverse that premium quickly. 🟢 Net favorable longer-term, watch closely.
