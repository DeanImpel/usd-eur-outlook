1. Bottom Line

Every dollar you convert today buys ~€0.876 — a small step down from yesterday, as the euro firmed marginally ahead of today's ECB rate decision even with Middle East tensions still elevated. The broader trend since February remains solidly in your favor (+3.6%); with the ECB deciding today (Jul 23) and the Fed next week (Jul 28–29), it's reasonable to hold off converting a larger tranche until the ECB outcome is known, since a hawkish September-hike signal could push the rate down further while a dovish hold could reverse today's dip back in your favor.

This remains the single biggest wildcard for both currencies, but today it's the ECB decision — happening this afternoon — that is the most immediate event risk.

3. What's Driving It

  • ECB rate decision — today's dominant factor: The ECB decides today, July 23, with a press conference the same day. Markets expect a hold at 2.25%, but pricing has shifted toward roughly a 70% chance of a September hike — the euro firmed modestly into the decision, pushing EUR/USD up to 1.1418. 🔴 Two-sided, the single biggest live event risk today.
  • Dollar holding near cycle highs, but not gaining today: The Dollar Index sits near ~101.12 (latest available print) on continued Hormuz-driven safe-haven demand and hawkish Fed positioning, but today's move was outweighed by the euro's pre-ECB firming. 🟡 Neutral today, structurally supportive of the dollar longer-term.
  • European gas prices — still elevated despite today's dip: TTF natural gas eased to €61.95/MWh (-0.96% d/d) but remains up ~50% over the past month and ~91% YoY, tied to Hormuz-driven LNG supply risk and heat-wave demand — a continuing cost drag on the eurozone economy. 🟡 Mildly unfavorable for the euro longer-term, but not today's driver.
  • Fed vs. ECB rate gap (still wide, still structurally favorable to you longer-term): Fed holds at 3.50–3.75%, next decision Jul 28–29; ECB decides today — a hold at 2.25% is expected, though a hawkish tone or explicit September-hike signal today would be euro-supportive and work against further € per $1 gains. 🔴 Two-sided, key event risk today.
  • Strait of Hormuz — still near-total shutdown: Non-Iranian ship transits fell to ~25 from 108 week-on-week, and the US struck an oil tanker near Iran's main export terminal overnight. A classic two-sided risk — further escalation typically favors the dollar via safe-haven flows, while any de-escalation could reverse that premium quickly. 🟢 Net favorable longer-term, watch closely.

APEX Take — Conversion Strategy

€ per $1 slipped slightly to €0.8758 today (-0.05%), as the euro firmed marginally ahead of this afternoon's ECB rate decision. You're still getting meaningfully more euro per dollar than you were in February (+3.6%), but with the ECB decision landing today and real potential to move the rate in either direction, converting a large tranche right this moment means taking on unnecessary event risk you could avoid by waiting a few hours.

Recommendation: Convert a smaller tranche now (e.g., 15–20%) at ~€0.876 per dollar to keep some conversion moving, and hold the bulk to convert once today's ECB decision and press conference are known — a hold-and-neutral outcome likely keeps the rate range-bound and safe to convert a larger chunk (30–40%) shortly after, while a hawkish September-hike signal argues for waiting further toward the July 28–29 FOMC meeting. If your bank or transfer provider offers a forward contract or rate alert/limit order, that remains a better tool than manual timing for the remaining balance.
