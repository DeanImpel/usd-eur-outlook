1. Bottom Line

Every dollar you convert today buys ~€0.876 — essentially flat over the past few sessions, but still up about 3.5% from February's low point. The favorable trend is holding, not accelerating, so there's no urgency to convert everything today.
  • Six months ago (Jan/Feb) $1 got you as little as roughly €0.829–€0.846. As of the July 10 close, $1 gets you €0.8760 — and the best single day so far this year was June 24, when $1 briefly bought €0.8806.
  • The improvement is driven by dollar strength: a hawkish Fed under new Chair Kevin Warsh, a still-wide (though narrowing) Fed–ECB interest-rate gap, and safe-haven demand tied to the escalating Strait of Hormuz conflict are all pushing more euro your way per dollar converted.
  • The rate has been range-bound just under the June 24 peak for the past week — read the chart below as "up = more euro for you."

3. What's Driving It

  • Fed vs. ECB rate gap (primary driver, narrowing): Fed holds at 3.50–3.75% with Chair Kevin Warsh reiterating July 1 at the Sintra forum that inflation remains "too high" — a hawkish signal. The ECB raised its deposit rate 25bp to 2.25% on June 11 (effective June 17), narrowing the gap to roughly ~125–150bp. Still dollar-favorable overall, but the direction of travel (ECB tightening) is a mild headwind versus earlier in the year. 🟢 Favorable, narrowing.
  • Dollar Index (DXY) strength: Sitting near 100.97, up 1.11% over the past month and 3.18% year-over-year — firm dollar continues to support your conversion rate. 🟢 Favorable.
  • Strait of Hormuz conflict: Sharply escalating — July 11 saw the worst single day of attacks since the June 17 US–Iran truce (US struck ~140 Iranian targets after three merchant vessels were hit this week, one crew member missing). Large-vessel crossings on the tracked shipping route have been at a near-standstill since July 7. Escalation drives safe-haven money into the dollar, meaning more euro per dollar for you. 🟢 Favorable.
  • Recent US labor/inflation data: No major surprise prints in the past few days; the market is now waiting on the June CPI release (July 14) for the next real catalyst. Fed messaging remains hawkish in the interim. 🟢 Mildly favorable.
  • Countervailing risk: If the Hormuz conflict de-escalates or a ceasefire holds, safe-haven dollar demand could unwind quickly, giving back some of the recent gain. A further ECB hike signal at the July 23 meeting would also work against you. This is the main risk to the current favorable trend. 🔴 Watch.

APEX Take — Conversion Strategy

The rate is holding its gains rather than extending them — € per $1 is essentially flat over the past several sessions after a strong run through late June. The Fed–ECB dynamic that drove the earlier improvement is now two-sided: the Fed stays hawkish, but the ECB's June 11 hike is a real (if modest) headwind, and the Hormuz situation could reverse the favorable safe-haven flow quickly if a ceasefire holds.

Recommendation: This is a reasonable, not exceptional, window. Convert a moderate portion now (e.g., 50%) at ~€0.876 per dollar to bank the current level, and hold the remainder to convert around July 14 (CPI) and July 23 (ECB decision) — both could move the rate meaningfully in either direction, and staging reduces the risk of converting a lump sum right before an unfavorable surprise. If your bank or transfer provider offers a forward contract or rate alert/limit order, that remains a better tool than manual timing for the remaining tranche.
